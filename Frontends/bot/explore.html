Cultural Facts

Discover fascinating insights about traditions, customs, and practices from Cameroon.

Food Culture

Nkui

Nkui is a popular Bamileke traditional dish, a sticky sauce made from back of tree, often cooked to celebrate special occasions like child's birth.

Architecture

Two Headed Snake

The two headed snake, a known architecture and a powerful symbol in Bamun culture, represents the dual power of the king and their ability to be strong on all fronts.

Language

Bamileke Language

The Bamileke people of Cameroon speak a variety of languages, all belonging to the broader category of Grassfeilds languages. These languages are part of the Southern Bantiod branch of the Niger-Congo language family. Some of the most prominent Bamileke languages include Ghomala, Fe'fe, Yemba, Medumba and Kwa.

Festivals

The Nguon Festival

An important tradition is the Nguon festival, a celebration where the king is put on trial by his people, a practice beleived to have originated from the Nguon owners who ensured good food supply and reported grievances during harvest time .

Art & Craft

The Bamileke Craftmanship

Bamileke art is renowned for its intricate designs and vibrant colors. They are particularly known for their wooden Masks, which are used in ceremonies to represent spirits of ancestors and deities. Other notable crafts include pottery, beadwork, and woven textiles.

Music and Dance

Music in the Nso

Music and Dance are intergral parts of Nso culture, with various dance groups performing to traditional music, often accompanied by drums and xylophones.

Social Customs

Social Structure

The Bassa are traditionally organized into independent clans with a lineage-based system, each with a chief. They have a strong sense of community and celebrate important occasions with rituals, dancing and feasting.

Spirituality

Totem

Totems are used in ceremonies, where family members swear they were not involved in the death of a loved one, with the belief that the totem will trap the spirit of a muderer.
